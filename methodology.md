# Risk Assessment Methodology

This risk assessment follows a structured cybersecurity risk management process commonly used in industry frameworks.

## Step 1: Asset Identification

Identify systems, data, and resources that have value to the organization.

Examples:
- Workstations
- File servers
- Email systems
- Cloud storage
- Network devices

## Step 2: Threat Identification

Identify potential threats that could exploit vulnerabilities.

Examples:
- Malware
- Phishing attacks
- Unauthorized access
- Data breaches
- Insider threats

## Step 3: Vulnerability Identification

Identify weaknesses that attackers could exploit.

Examples:
- Weak passwords
- Unpatched software
- Lack of multi-factor authentication
- Misconfigured firewalls

## Step 4: Risk Analysis

Risk is calculated using:

Risk = Likelihood × Impact

## Step 5: Risk Evaluation

Risks are prioritised based on severity to determine which risks require immediate mitigation.

## Step 6: Risk Treatment

Security controls and mitigation strategies are recommended to reduce risk exposure.
